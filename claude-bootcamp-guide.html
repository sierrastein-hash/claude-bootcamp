6

your data is yours

the question everyone asks: "is my stuff safe?" here's the short version:

→ Anthropic does not train on your conversations by default
→ connections (Gmail, Calendar) are authorized by you and can be revoked anytime in Settings → Connections
→ Claude can only access what you explicitly give it — files you upload, apps you connect, text you type
→ conversations are stored in your account. delete anytime from the sidebar

the rule: Claude sees what you show it. nothing more. if you disconnect Gmail, it loses access immediately.

make it yours

profile, memory, and voice — teach Claude who you are

1

set your profile

Settings → Profile. name + role + what you work on + how you like things written. this carries into every conversation.

example
"i'm Sierra, a CSM at a SaaS company. i manage 30 enterprise accounts — QBR prep, customer emails, stakeholder updates. concise and warm, not corporate."
2

turn on memory

Settings → Memory → on. Claude remembers across conversations. say "remember my team uses Notion" or "forget that" to manage it.

3

train your writing voice

PRO

the most impactful thing you can set up. three steps:

generate: paste 3–5 sent emails into a chat (or connect Gmail in Cowork for 30+ emails automatically):

prompt
"analyze my writing style — tone, formality, sentence structure, how I open/close, phrases I default to. create a writing style profile I can save."

save: copy the profile → Settings → Writing Style → Create Custom → paste → save. don't just read it and close the chat.

result: every conversation now sounds like you, not AI.

free plan: paste your profile at the start of important conversations: "use this writing style: [paste]"

Claude as your chief of staff

the most valuable setup — turn Claude into someone who runs your day

one-time setup (15 min)

1. connect Gmail + Calendar — Settings → Connections (Claude only reads what you authorize — revoke anytime)
2. save your writing style (see "make it yours")
3. create a "chief of staff" project PRO:

system prompt
"you're my chief of staff. I'm [name], [role] at [company]. priorities: [2-3 things]. check my calendar and email for briefings. match my writing style. be direct, flag what's urgent, skip fluff."
free plan: paste context at the start of each session: "you're my chief of staff today. here's my context: [paste]."

your daily rhythms

morning briefing

start every day here.
prompt
"morning briefing. what's on my calendar today? for each meeting — who's there, what should I know. check email — anything urgent before my first call? anything I haven't responded to?"

email triage

sort your inbox, draft replies.
prompt
"go through unread emails. sort: (1) needs response today, (2) can wait, (3) FYI. draft replies for urgent ones in my voice. flag anything sensitive."

meeting prep

one-page brief before any important call.
prompt
"meeting with [person] at 2pm. search recent news, check our email history. prep: who they are, what we last discussed, what to bring up, risks."

end-of-day wrap

surface commitments, prep tomorrow.
prompt
"what did I commit to today? draft follow-up emails I owe. what's first tomorrow?"

weekly review (friday)

look ahead, catch dropped balls.
prompt
"weekly review. next week's calendar — what needs prep? any conflicts? anything from this week I said I'd do but haven't?"
compound effect: with memory on, Claude learns your priorities, rhythms, and who matters. after two weeks it's noticeably more tailored. automate: "create a scheduled task for my morning briefing every weekday at 8am."

your workspace

projects, connections, and skills — the deeper configuration

1

projects

PRO

persistent workspaces. set context once — every chat inside already knows. add knowledge files (PDFs, docs, spreadsheets) for Claude to reference.

set up: sidebar → Projects → New Project → system prompt + upload files.

example: QBR prep
"you help me prep QBRs. I'm a CSM. include: usage metrics, health score, wins, risks, next steps. confident, not salesy. always include a slide-ready summary."
power move: start a chat first: "help me brainstorm what context you'd need to be the perfect assistant for [task]. ask me questions." use that to write your prompt.
2

connections (MCP)

how Claude plugs into your tools. Settings → Connections → authorize apps. or just ask Claude to do something that needs one and it'll prompt you.

Gmail — read, draft, search, style analysis
Calendar — schedule, prep, free time
Slack — channels, threads, drafts
Notes / iMessage — read, create, send
3

skills

built-in: Word, PowerPoint, Excel, PDF — just ask naturally. "write me a proposal" → .docx. "build a deck" → .pptx.

custom: for anything you repeat. "create a skill called 'weekly-report' — wins, blockers, priorities. 3-5 items each, direct tone." Claude auto-detects it later, or you say "use my weekly-report skill."

projects vs skills: projects = context (what to know). skills = instructions (how to do it). use both.

writing cheat sheet

tell Claude who's reading

same info, different audience = completely different output. "email to my VP — data-driven, 4 sentences" vs "Slack to my team — casual, just the key changes."

the formula

role + task + audience + format + constraints. "CSM, follow-up email, customer who went silent, warm but professional, max 100 words."

ask for options

"give me 3 versions" — never accept draft one. ask for variations in tone, length, or approach. pick the best, iterate from there.

ask questions first

"before you start, ask me 5 questions" — Claude will ask what it needs to nail it. way better than a vague prompt and a mediocre first draft.

demand accuracy

"confirm this is 100% accurate before giving me the final" — Claude will double-check its own work. for anything with numbers, facts, or names — always ask it to verify.

iterate, don't accept

the power-user secret: never accept the first draft. say "make it shorter" · "try a warmer tone" · "keep paragraphs 2-3 but rewrite the opening." each round gets dramatically better. 3 rounds beats 1 perfect prompt every time.

quick tricks

"be concise" · "no preamble" · "shorter" · "push back on me" · "what's missing?" — all work exactly like you'd expect.

when things go wrong

common problems and how to fix them

too long / too generic

"be concise" or "max 3 sentences" or just "shorter." add "no preamble, no summary" to cut filler. more specifics in your prompt = less guessing.

sounds like AI

quick fix: "write like a real person." permanent fix: train your voice and save a custom style. the words "delve" and "leverage" disappear once you do.

misunderstood me

don't start over. "no, I meant..." or "keep everything but change..." Claude adjusts fast.

hit the limit

free: wait a few hours. pro: switch to Haiku (separate limit). fewer, more detailed messages beats rapid back-and-forth.

lost the thread

Cmd+N for new topics. re-state context in long convos. use projects PRO to persist context.

conversation hygiene

the most common beginner mistake: using one giant chat for everything. here's how to think about it:

→ new topic = new chat (Cmd+N). Claude doesn't carry context between conversations unless you use Memory or Projects
→ same task, keep going. "make it shorter" / "try a different angle" / "add a section on X" — all work great in the same chat
→ long chats lose context. after 20+ back-and-forths, Claude may forget what you said early on. start fresh and re-paste key context
→ your history lives in the sidebar. every conversation is saved — search it anytime. delete what you don't need

Cowork went off track

just delete the file — it's a normal file on your computer. say "that's not what I wanted" to redirect. you can also type corrections while Cowork is still working.

agents

AI that acts, not just answers
"agent" = AI that does things autonomously — browsing, reading files, creating docs, chaining steps. the "agent startup" hype is the same idea at scale. you don't build agents — you use one every time you use Cowork.
prompt tip: describe the end result, not the steps. "find emails needing replies, draft responses" not "open Gmail, then read each one, then..."
can't do: access apps you haven't connected · irreversible actions without approval · background monitoring · be 100% accurate (always review)
→ you're already using agents in Cowork
